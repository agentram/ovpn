# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Participants in this project are expected to keep discussions constructive, technical, and respectful.

Examples of acceptable behavior:

- assuming good intent while debating technical tradeoffs
- giving concrete, reproducible feedback
- focusing criticism on code, behavior, and design rather than people
- respecting privacy and never posting secrets or private infrastructure details

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- doxxing or sharing private information
- spam, trolling, or deliberately disruptive behavior
- posting credentials, tokens, private keys, or sensitive operational data

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content that violates this policy, and may restrict participation when needed to protect the project and its contributors.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to issues, discussions, pull requests, review comments, and other project spaces.

## Reporting

If you encounter abusive behavior or a privacy/safety problem, contact the maintainers through GitHub moderation tools or private vulnerability reporting when security-sensitive data is involved.
